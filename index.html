THE 3 PROBLEMS WITH IN-PERSON TRAINING
Surface the real cost of staying put — with empathy, not criticism.
🎙 Script — 3 Problems
3:30 – 6:00
Okay, before I get into how to actually build this — I want to name the three structural problems with the in-person model. Not because I want to make you feel bad about where you are. But because I want you to see clearly why the model itself has a ceiling — and it's not your fault.

Problem one: You are underpaid and overworked.
The average personal trainer in the US earns between $50K and $65K a year. Working 40 to 50 hours a week. When you factor in cancellations, no-shows, and all the dead time between sessions — your effective hourly rate is $25 to $35 an hour. That's barely better than a skilled trade. And trading your time for money is the worst trade you can make. We can always make more money. We cannot make more time — especially if you have a family.

Problem two: You are legally capped on what you can actually provide.
Most PTs cannot legally coach on nutrition without additional certification. But here's the thing — nutrition is where your clients' results actually live. You're helping with workouts. Your clients are left alone on everything else. That's not a knock on you — it's a structural limit of the in-person model. Inside IFCA, we give you the actual ISSA and AFAA-accredited nutrition certs so you can fix this.

Problem three: You only get paid when you are physically present.
Client cancels — you don't get paid. You get sick — you don't get paid. You take a vacation — you don't get paid, AND you're spending money at the same time. And God forbid we ever see another 2020.

One of our clients, Adam Badger, had been training in person for years. Early mornings, split shifts, weekends. He pivoted online with us. And a few months in — he was home on a Friday afternoon at 1pm. His wife and son were out of town. And he made $3,000 from a text conversation with a current client using a simple upsell framework. No sales call. 1pm on a Friday, from his couch.

That is not possible in the in-person model. And it's completely normal in ours.

STOP SELLING "ONLINE PERSONAL TRAINING"
The offer reframe — why the method/result distinction changes everything including your price.
🎙 Script — The Offer Reframe
8:30 – 11:30
Okay, here's where I see most trainers who try to go online get stuck — and this is the most important concept in this whole episode so I need you to hear this.

When trainers go online, the first thing they do is call it "online personal training." Same service, just delivered through a screen. And now they're competing with hundreds of thousands of coaches who are all charging $97 to $149 a month.

You've just commoditized yourself the moment you went online.

Here's the thing. When someone walks into a gym, they are not thinking "I want a personal training package." They want to lose 30 pounds. They want to stop their back from hurting. They want to be around for their kids. People don't want workouts. They want the result workouts produce.

Jordan has this analogy that I love. Wrong way: "Hey, eat your broccoli — it's good for you." Nobody wants to hear that. Right way: "Do you want to grow up big and strong like dad?" — "Yes!" — "Then the secret is eating your broccoli every single day." Same broccoli. Completely different buy-in.

The process stays the same. The framing changes everything.

Look at what this does to your price. "I sell online personal training" — $99 to $149 a month. Commodity. You're competing with literally everyone. But if you say "I help adults over 40 lose 15 to 25 pounds, optimize their hormones, and have the energy to actually show up for their family — through my Longevity Multiplier Method" — that's $1,000 a month. Same trainer. Same workouts. Same delivery platform.

The difference is entirely in how you've framed what you're selling. And at $1,000 a month, you need 15 clients for $15K a month. At $149, you'd need over 100. Which one sounds more sustainable to you?

THE THREE-STAGE LEAD ENGINE
The exact sequence of how clients get built — and why the order matters.
🎙 Script — Three-Stage Lead Engine
14:00 – 17:30
Now let me show you exactly how this comes together — because the sequence matters more than almost anything else here.

Stage one is the Affinity Launch. This is structured warm outreach to your existing network. Not cold DMs to strangers. Not posting into the void. Reconnecting with people who already know you, using a conversation framework that feels completely natural — not like a pitch.

Out of every 100 contacts you reach out to, typically 3 to 7 become clients. At $3,000 a package, that's $9,000 to $21,000 before a single ad dollar ever gets spent. These first clients do three things: they prove your offer, they fund your content, and they create social proof. You now have real results to talk about.

Stage two is the AI Content System. While your Affinity Launch is running, we build and systematize your content. This is not a daily posting grind. It's targeted content that speaks directly to your ideal client — testing what actually resonates organically. Some coaches get inbound leads before they ever touch ads. Either way, you're building the audience that will make ads incredibly effective when you turn them on.

Stage three is Paid Amplification. Once the offer is proven — you have clients, you have results, you know what message lands — ads go on to scale what already works. Not before. The biggest mistake I see is trainers running ads to a broken offer with a cold pixel. By the time we turn on ads, you already know what converts.

One of our students, Chris Braley — in-person trainer and gym owner, was working 50 to 60 hours a week. Joined IFCA. Within 19 weeks he had done $43,000 in collected cash, $48,000 in contracted revenue. And he didn't lose a single in-person client. He upgraded every one of them to his online program.

That's the sequence. Network proves the offer. Content tests messaging. Ads scale what's proven. Reverse the order and you're just burning money.
